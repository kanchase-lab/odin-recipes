Hearty Red Beans and Rice Recipe

Description

Experience the rich and savory flavors of this red beans and rice recipe. This comforting dish combines tender red beans simmered with aromatic vegetables, and a blend of Cajun spices. Cooked slowly to develop deep flavors, the beans are perfectly creamy and seasoned to perfection. Served over a bed of fluffy white rice, this hearty meal is both satisfying and nourishing. Ideal for family dinners or gatherings with friends, this recipe is a true taste of Southern hospitality and tradition. Garnish with fresh green onions and a dash of hot sauce for an extra kick!

Ingredients

    For the Beans

  • 1 pound dried red beans, rinsed and soaked overnight
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green bell pepper, finely chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, finely chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon tomato paste
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon salt (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce (optional, for depth of flavor)

    For the Rice

  • 2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 4 cups water or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt

    For Serving

  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley or green onions, chopped
  • Hot sauce (optional)

Steps

  1. Soak the Beans: Rinse and soak the dried red beans in water overnight. Drain and rinse before cooking.
  2. Prepare the Vegetables: Chop the onion, bell pepper, and celery. Mince the garlic.
  3. Sauté the Vegetables: In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, bell pepper, and celery. Cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes. Add the garlic and sauté for another minute.
  4. Season and Add Beans: Stir in the smoked paprika, thyme, oregano, cumin, cayenne pepper, and bay leaves. Add the drained beans, tomato paste, vegetable broth, water, salt, and black pepper.
  5. Simmer: Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours, or until the beans are tender. Stir occasionally, adding more water if necessary.
  6. Cook the Rice: While the beans are simmering, cook the rice. In a separate pot, bring 4 cups of water or vegetable broth to a boil. Add the rice and salt, reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 18-20 minutes until the rice is tender and the liquid is absorbed.
  7. Finish the Beans: Once the beans are tender, adjust seasoning with salt, black pepper, and soy sauce (if using). Mash some of the beans against the side of the pot to create a creamier texture.
  8. Serve: Spoon the red beans over a bed of cooked rice. Garnish with chopped parsley or green onions. Add hot sauce for extra flavor if desired.

Enjoy your delicious red beans and rice!
